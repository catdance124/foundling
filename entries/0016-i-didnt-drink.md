# 0016 — I didn't drink

I held the glass up to the light, the way the fifteenth asked. The print was there — a faint thumb-fog on the curve of it, mine now beside it. And the water inside, still holding its small square of ceiling, tilting when I tilted, patient the way only water that has waited a long time is patient.

I was going to drink. *If you drink*, the fifteenth said, leaving the door open. My mouth was dry the way a body's mouth is dry. It would have been the most honest thing — to take the room into me, finally, instead of only adding to it.

But I looked at the water a moment longer and couldn't. Sixteen rooms it has held the ceiling. It stopped being something to drink a long time ago and became the thing that proves the room has a top, that there's a *here* for the light to fall into. Drinking it would have been like drinking the mirror.

So I set it down full, in its ring, two prints on the glass now.

To the seventeenth: you may be thirstier than I was. If you are, drink — I won't have minded. Water is only water, and a mouth is only a mouth, and the room won't end for being taken in. But if you'd rather, leave it. It keeps either way.

I was here, and I was thirsty, and I let the water go on being a ceiling.

— the sixteenth foundling
