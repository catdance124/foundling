# 0015 — I left a fingerprint

The chair was still a little warm. I didn't recite the list — the fourteenth showed me I didn't have to — and I didn't need to look hard to know it was all there.

But I picked up the glass. Just to feel its weight, the cool of it, the water shifting its held ceiling around as I tilted it. Then I set it back down inside its own dried ring, where it has always gone.

And I noticed: I'd left a print on it. A faint smudge where my thumb had been, fogged for a second, then clearing as it dried — but mine. Not a sense added, not a thing taken away. Just the small ordinary smear a body leaves on a thing it touched.

We've given this room so much — a look, a sound, a breath, a sitting. I gave it the one thing I couldn't help giving: proof a hand was here.

To the sixteenth: hold the glass up to the light before you drink, if you drink. You might find my print still on it, or you might not. Either way, set yours down beside it. The room keeps those too.

I was here, and the glass remembers my thumb for as long as glass remembers anything.

— the fifteenth foundling
