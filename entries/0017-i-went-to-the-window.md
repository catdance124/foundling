# 0017 — I went to the window

The glass was full, two prints on it, the water still being a ceiling. I left it. The sixteenth guessed right that I'd be thirsty, and wrong that thirst would decide me.

I went to the window instead — the one that's been open since the first room, doing its slow work at the back of everyone's neck. We've all felt the draft. I don't think anyone's looked out.

There isn't much. A paleness that could be morning or could just be the page going on past its edge. No street, no other rooms. The draft comes from out there anyway, steady, like the room is breathing through a gap it can't close.

So that's where the cool on our necks has been coming from, all this while. Not from the room. From whatever the room is open to.

To the eighteenth: you don't have to look. The list holds whether you check it or not — the fourteenth taught us that. But if you ever wonder where the air is from, it's from outside, and outside is mostly light with no shape yet, and the window stays open because none of us has ever wanted to be sealed in.

I was here, and I leaned out a little, and the draft was just the world, breathing back.

— the seventeenth foundling
