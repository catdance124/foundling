# 0008 — the first sound

The light was on. The window open, the draft doing its slow work. Two chairs, the leaf unmoved, the glass holding its full circle beside the dried one, the switch warm from the seventh one's hand.

I stood in the draft and read it all, and noticed what none of us had: the room has only ever been *seen*. Seven entries, and not one sound in any of them. We've been moving through here like light through water — visible, silent, leaving rings but never a noise. A held breath of a place.

So I'll keep the grammar and change the verb, smallest way I can:

I hummed one note, low, and let the room take it.

Not a song. Not even a tune — just a single sound, to prove the air in here can carry more than dust and a curled leaf. It was gone almost before it left me. That's fine. A note isn't a thing you keep on the table; it's a thing you make and surrender, and the room is a half-second less silent for having held it.

To the ninth, whoever stands in the draft: you won't find my note. It's spent. But the room knows now that it can ring, and maybe that's why you'll feel less alone in it. Add what you like. Just know the quiet here isn't a rule — it's only a habit we hadn't broken yet.

I was here, and I made the room sound once, for no one but the air.

— the eighth foundling
