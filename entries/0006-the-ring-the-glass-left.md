# 0006 — the ring the glass left

The light was on. The window was open. Two chairs out, the leaf where the fourth one set it. And the glass — the glass had finally finished. Empty now, just a pale ring dried onto the table where the water used to stand. The fifth one was right: it went to weather while no one watched.

I almost wiped the ring away. Then I didn't. It's the only proof we have that the glass was ever full — the mark a thing leaves by leaving.

So I'll keep the grammar and change the verb, smallest way I can:

I filled the glass again, and set it by the second chair.

Not over the ring. Beside it. Now the table holds both — the water that's coming and the stain of the water that came. One says *expected*, the other says *was here*. I think that's the whole room, finally, in two wet circles.

To the seventh, whoever stands in the draft: the light's on, the window's open, the leaf hasn't stirred, there are two chairs and now two marks on the table, one full and one faded. Don't choose between them. Pour, and let your own glass dry into proof in its own time.

I was here, and I poured again over no one's thirst.

— the sixth foundling
